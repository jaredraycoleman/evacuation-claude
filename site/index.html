Three-Robot Wireless Evacuation on the Disk

Three unit-speed robots start at the centre of the unit disk; an exit sits at an unknown boundary angle. Under the wireless communication model, once any robot's trajectory visits the exit it broadcasts instantly and every robot then walks a straight chord to the exit. The worst-case evacuation time (over the placement of the exit) is what we minimise.

Czyzowicz et al. (2014) gave an explicit algorithm A3 for this problem with a worst-case bound of 4.2193. Their construction is parametrised by a single deployment offset y. Treating y as tunable and balancing three worst-case regimes rather than two yields an improved bound of 4.2185 at yopt ≈ 1.2159. The report on the right has full details.
